Introduction to Chemistry

Chemical Reactions

Chemistry is the scientific study of matter, its properties, and how substances combine or separate to form other substances. It’s commonly known as the central science because it bridges physics and biology. In this field, chemical reactions are a major focus.

In chemistry, a chemical reaction is a process that leads to the transformation of one set of chemical substances to another.

Chemical Formula Example

One of the most common reactions is the combination of hydrogen 2 (H2) and oxygen 2 (O2) to form water (H2O).

Variables in Chemistry

In chemical equations, variables like n (number of moles), V (volume), and T (temperature) are used to describe the system's state.

Avogadro's Number

Avogadro's number is 6.022 × 1023, which represents the number of atoms or molecules in one mole of a substance.
